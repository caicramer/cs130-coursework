Primary Type:

Open

In general, states that do not ask voters to choose parties on the voter registration form are “open primary” states. In an open primary, voters may choose privately in which primary to vote. In other words, voters may choose which party’s ballot to vote on, but this decision is private and does not register the voter with that party. This permits a voter to cast a vote across party lines for the primary election. Critics argue that the open primary weakens the parties’ ability to nominate. Supporters say this system gives voters maximal flexibility, allowing them to cross party lines and maintain their privacy.
